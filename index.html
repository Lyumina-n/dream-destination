CAPPADOCIA

The land you'll always remember!

Turkish Coffee

Turkish is a must try! During the Ottoman period, women socialized with each other over coffee and sweets. Men socialized in coffee houses to discuss politics and to play backgammon (Tavla in Turkish). Today, Turkish coffee houses continue their role in society as a meeting place for both the cultured citizen and the inquisitive traveler. Turkish coffee is served hot from a special coffee pot called "cezve". The flavor is all about Turkey, not ordinary, like sand and the sea the same time. After you finish your cup turn it upside down on the saucer and let it cool for fortune reading from the coffee grounds remaining in the cup.

Turkish pastry

Pide, Lahmacun, Poğaça, Börek traditional lavash bread, but your heart will be broken by Gözleme. Typical in rural areas, made of lavash bread or phyllo dough folded around a variety of fillings such as spinach, cheese and parsley, minced meat or potatoes and cooked on a large griddle (you can spend hours just watching).

Specialty

Testi (Pottery) Kebab is a popular local dish cooked with lamb or beef meat, onion, pepper, garlic, tomatoes, potatoes, and spices in a unique jug, that is literally on fire.
Cappadocia is the perfect place to try the best lamb.

Turkish delights

Turkish Baklava is the most famous traditional Turkish dessert that people around the world know and enjoy.
Tavuk Gogsu is translated as "Chicken Breast" in English, the main ingredient in this pudding. First, the chicken is boiled and shredded into fibers. Later it is again boiled with water, sugar, milk, rice, or cornstarch. Once it is ready, cinnamon is used for flavoring.
Kunefe is made like a cake which is later cut into pieces. You will not find it among pastries as it should be eaten hot.
